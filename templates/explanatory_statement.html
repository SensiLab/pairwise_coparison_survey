This is an invitation to participate in this study. Please read this Explanatory Statement carefully and in full before deciding whether or not to take part in this research. If you would require further information regarding any aspect of this project, you are encouraged to contact the researchers via the email address provided above.

What does the research involve?

We are conducting an online survey that involves answering simple questions about computer-generated abstract images.
The survey is open ended, and you can answer as many questions as you like.

Why were you selected to participate?

This survey is open to all members of the public.

Consenting to participate and withdrawing from the research.

Before you begin the survey you will be asked to read a consent form on your web browser, followed by three questions regarding age, gender and your level of creative proficiency. You do not need to answer these questions nor accept the conditions of the consent form if you do not wish to continue, and even if you accept, you can leave the experiment at any time. No explanations or justifications are required.
The data collected in this experiment will be anonymous, which means that once you have submitted your answers, they will not be associated with you in any way, and therefore cannot be deleted upon request.

Possible benefits and risks to participants.

Your participation will help us understand how people appreciate complex and interesting images.

Confidentiality.

All the information collected in this survey will be anonymised. Additionally, no IP address or any other digital signature that can associate you with your participation will be collected nor stored.

Storage of Data.

All the data collected during this study will be stored in a server located at Monash University. Anonymous, un-aggregated data will be published online accompanying academic publications.

Results.

When results obtained through this experiment are used in a publication, the details of the publication will be listed on the SensiLab website, which can be accessed at any time.

Complaints.

Should you have any concerns or complaints regarding the way this project is conducted, you are welcome to contact the Executive Officer, Monash University Human Research Ethics (MUHREC).
